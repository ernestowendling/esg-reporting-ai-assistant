# Synthetic Green Transition Fund — Sustainability Policy

## Scope and status

This fictional policy applies to the Synthetic Green Transition Fund. It was created solely for a public software demonstration and contains no client, employer or regulated production data.

## Environmental objective

The fund promotes climate-change mitigation through investments in issuers with credible transition plans. The portfolio-level target is to reduce weighted-average carbon intensity by 30% between the synthetic 2023 baseline and the end of 2028.

## Exclusions

The fund excludes issuers deriving more than 5% of revenue from thermal-coal extraction. It also excludes issuers involved in controversial weapons, including anti-personnel mines, cluster munitions, chemical weapons and biological weapons.

## Engagement and escalation

Portfolio companies with material climate-transition gaps enter an engagement programme. After two unsuccessful annual engagement cycles, the case must be escalated to the Sustainability Committee. The Committee may approve an intensified engagement plan, voting action, position reduction or divestment.

## Governance and review

The investment team owns first-line implementation. The ESG Oversight function performs second-line challenge each quarter. Material exceptions are documented, assigned to an owner and reported to the Fund Governance Committee. All public disclosures require human review and approval.
